Summary

The Godfather is a 1972 crime film directed by Francis Ford Coppola. It tells the story of the powerful Italian-American crime family of Don Vito Corleone. When the Don's youngest son, Michael, joins the mafia, a tale of family, loyalty, and betrayal unfolds.
